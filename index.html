2010

The Chennai Super Kings were one of the eight teams that took part in the 2010 Indian Premier League. They were captained by Indian skipper Mahendra Singh Dhoni for the third year in succession. They won the tournament after beating Mumbai Indians by 22 runs in the finals. With this, they qualified for the 2010 Champions League Twenty20, which they won by beating the Warriors in the finals.

2011

The Super Kings completed a hat-trick of titles by winning the 2011 Indian Premier League. Thus, they became the first team in the history of IPL to successfully defend their title. They also won all the matches held at their home ground in Chennai that season and created a new record of becoming the first IPL team to win all of its home games. With this, they earned a direct qualification for the main event of 2011 Champions League Twenty20 where they could not replicate similar consistency before crashing out of the event in the group stages.

Franchise history

In September 2007, the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) announced the establishment of the Indian Premier League, a Twenty20 competition to be started in 2008.[5] In January 2008, the BCCI unveiled the owners of eight city-based franchises. The Chennai franchise was sold to the India Cements for $91 million, making it the fourth most expensive team in the league behind Mumbai, Bangalore and Hyderabad.[6] India Cements acquired the rights to the franchise for 10 years. Former ICC Chairman N. Srinivasan was the de facto owner of the Chennai Super Kings, by means of his position as the vice-chairman and managing director of India Cements Ltd. The franchisee was transferred to a separate entity named Chennai Super Kings Cricket Ltd., after the Supreme Court of India struck down the controversial amendment to the BCCI constitution's clause 6.2.4 that allowed board officials to have a commercial interest in the IPL and the Champions League Twenty20 on 22 January 2015.[7]

CSK FAQ: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS.

  • WHAT IS THE COLLEGE SEARCH KICKOFF?

    College Search Kickoff (CSK) is an invitational showcase for youth soccer players who want to play on the college level and are getting ready to enter their junior or senior year in high school in the fall 2021. We use the high school graduating year as the basis of team age. Eligible teams are players (seniors, juniors, sophomores, freshman and some eighth graders) with the graduating years of 2021, 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025 and 2026. Equivalent to U19, U18, U17, U16, U15 and U14, club and league select teams registered for the 2020-2021 seasonal year are eligible. We may consider U14 teams with eighth grade players graduating year 2026. No younger players graduating after 2026.

  • WHAT ARE THE RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE CSK TEAM MANAGER?

    CSK TEAM MANAGER is responsible for ALL communication with CSK officials regarding the application process as well as the timely distribution of CSK and college information to players and parents. They are also required to prepare and submit online a CSK Team Roster NO LATER than the deadline date, collect a completed medical waiver from each player, and have all required items when they attend team check-in during the time designated prior to the team's first game at the Muscatine Soccer Complex.

  • DO ALL INVITED CSK TEAMS HAVE TO SUBMIT A CSK TEAM ROSTER?

    Yes. CSK Team Rosters will be distributed in a book to college coaches attending the College Search Kickoff. If your team is invited to CSK, your CSK TEAM MANAGER will receive an email by the first week of July. The email will include instructions on how to prepare a CSK Team Roster. A standard format is important for the accuracy, completeness and ease-of-use of this player reference. Data on your entire team is listed on a single page. Completing the CSK Player Data form for each player on your team before school dismisses in the spring will help your team meet the deadline.
